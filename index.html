UCL/YUNG YiDiSH Spring School  in Contemporary Spoken Yiddish and Hasidic Culture

Tel Aviv, 10-16 May 2020

a week-long immersion course for intermediate and advanced learners in spoken Yiddish learning about contemporary Hasidic culture

LEARN MORE

AT A GLANCE

  Our Contemporary Spoken Yiddish immersion course will be all in Yiddish. It is necessary that you speak Yiddish at least an intermediate level and that you are interested in improving your spoken and listening skills. There is less of a focus on reading and writing skills. For this reason we include the information, for now, all in English. But a Spoken Yiddish and Hebrew version is in the pipeline.

Morning

We will spend mornings together in the library with a Yiddish native speaker, doing volunteering work in the library. You can also have private study time exploring the books in the collection. Coffee/tea and light food will be available all day.

Afternoon

Mendy Cahan will teach the group for three hours every afternoon about Hasidism and contemporary Hasidic Yiddish culture. Professor Kriszta Eszter Szendroi and Dr Lily Kahn from UCL  will discuss the gramar of Hasidic Yiddish. Other teachers are to be confirmed. UCL can provide a certificate of attendance for university students, if required. 

Evening

In the evenings, there will be public lectures, concerts, cabarets and other performances at YUNG YiDiSH, even a Hasidic badkhen! Your attendance is complimentary; a friend travelling with you can attend the events at a reduced rate. 

LagBeOmer

We will travel to the Meron for an overnight trip to take part in the so-called Hasidic Burning Man, where hundreds of thousands of Haredi people gather at LagBeOmer to sing, dance and celebrate together throughout the night. 

Shabes

We will mark the incoming of the shabes, and have no formal teaching on the day of the shabes. But we will gather for shalesides at YUNG YiDiSH and close our week together with making havdule and a throw a good-bye party on motse shabes.

Swinging Tel Aviv

Tel Aviv has everything to offer from perfect weather, to great cafes and bars, souks and of course, the Mediterranean...

More details

Spring School in Contemporary Spoken Yiddish and Hasidic Culture 2020 is co-organised by the HASYID research project from University College London and the YUNG YiDiSH Library & Cultural Centre in Tel Aviv.  










Prof Kriszta Eszter Szendroi & Dr Lily Kahn

Funded by the Arts and Humanties Research Council of the UK, the UCL research group on the grammar of Contemporary Hasidic Yiddish studies Hasidic Yiddish varieties all around the world. With our colleagues, Dr Zoë Belk, Dr Sonya Yampolskaya and our research assistants, we collect and analyse data from contemporary Hasidic speakers. Ours is the first large-scale attempt to document and study in depth the most widely-spoken contemporary variant of the language.

read more about the UCL HASIDYID project

MENDY CAHAN

Mendy was born and raised in a Yiddish-speaking family in Antverpen, and later moved to Israel to study literature and philosophy. He is an expert on contemporary Haredi Yiddish culture. He has taught Yiddish language and culture at various international summer courses.




Learning activities


The group will spend the mornings at YUNG YiDiSH Tel Aviv together, talking Yiddish at all times! You can either volunteer your time to help with uncatalogued new arrivals in the library or use this time to explore the collection in your individual study time. Cardamom coffee from Jaffa, tea, water and some light food will always be available, as will a native Hasidic speaker to chat with at all times.

In the afternoons, there will be three-hour formal classes on various topics, including Hasidism and contemporary Hasidic culture by Mendy Cahan, the grammar of Hasidic varieties of Yiddish by Kriszta Eszter Szendroi and Lily Kahn (UCL) and the women's perspective on Hasidism (tbc).




Evening cultural activities

There will be several lectures, concerts, cabarets taking place at YUNG YiDiSH during the Spring School including a performance by a Hasidic badkhen! Your attendance is free of charge. If you are travelling together with a friend who is not attending the school, we offer reduced ticket prices for them.

LagBeOmer at the Meron

Hundreds of thousands of Haredi Jews come together every year at LagBeOmer at the Meron. We will drive up in the afternoon, in time for the lighting of the fire by the Boyan Rebbe. We will dance, sing and immerse in this truly unique Hasidic experience throughout the night. We will arrive back in Tel Aviv in the morning and classes resume late in the afternoon.

Shabes





We will mark the arrival of the shabes together and we will have no formal classes on the shabes. But we will meet in the afternoon to eat, drink and sing together by a tish. We will mark the end of the shabes and our week together by making havdule. The school does not observe the shabes, but if you do, we will accommodate you.





Party time

To mark the end of the school and our week together, we will meet for shalesshides on shabes, make havdule and throw a party together on motse shabes
